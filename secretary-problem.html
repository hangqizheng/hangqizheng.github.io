What apartment hunting can teach you about optimal decision making: a look into the secretary problem

Sequential problems are pervasive, from apartment hunting to hiring candidates. Humans encounter these complex cognitive tasks daily, internalize them, and react accordingly. Understanding how people make decisions in the era of human-computer interaction is becoming increasingly important. Drawing from the secretary problem, I explored historical studies in this space and used statistical simulations to model decision-making. Understanding cognitive processes and decision-making serves a solid theoretical foundation for addressing critical informational challenges and designing interfaces that help humans formulate information entities with context awareness.
